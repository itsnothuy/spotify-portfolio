## Show the decision, not just the deliverable

A strong portfolio case study does more than list technologies. It explains:

- what problem mattered
- why the approach was chosen
- what tradeoffs were accepted
- what changed after the work shipped

That is usually the missing layer between "I built this" and "I know how to think."

## A simple structure that works

Use the following outline when you document a project:

### Context

Who was the work for, and what constraint made it interesting?

### Approach

What architecture, interaction model, or product decision carried the work?

### Outcome

What improved, shipped, or became easier because of the project?

## Keep the writing concrete

Specificity beats volume. A short paragraph with a real decision is stronger than three vague paragraphs about impact.

> People remember the constraints you handled more than the feature list you shipped.
